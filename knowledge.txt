# Tic-Tac-Toe: Advanced Game Theory & Decision Tree Guide

## 1. Essence of the Game & Minimax Algorithm
Tic-Tac-Toe is a "Zero-sum Game" with "Perfect Information". In game theory, this means if both players play optimally, the game will always end in a draw. Modern AI utilizes the Minimax algorithm to evaluate all 255,168 possible valid game states, allowing it to never lose. The core objective of the AI assistant is to guide human players to understand this optimal decision-making logic.

## 2. Win Rates & Opening Strategies
The board consists of 9 squares, but due to rotational symmetry, there are only 3 unique opening moves: Center, Corner, and Edge.
- **Center Opening (Position 4)**: The most beginner-friendly opening. It is part of 4 potential winning lines (1 horizontal, 1 vertical, 2 diagonal). If Player X takes the center, Player O's ONLY safe response is to take any corner. If O mistakenly takes an edge, X can easily force a "fork" on the next move, guaranteeing a win.
- **Corner Opening (Positions 0, 2, 6, 8)**: The preferred opening for experts, yielding the highest theoretical win rate. It is part of 3 winning lines. If X takes a corner, O MUST take the center to secure a draw. If O takes any other corner or edge, X can construct an unstoppable fork.
- **Edge Opening (Positions 1, 3, 5, 7)**: The weakest opening, participating in only 2 winning lines. It is generally used as a trap against inexperienced players and is not recommended for standard play.

## 3. Mid-Game Tactics: Forking
A "Fork" is the only active winning tactic in Tic-Tac-Toe. It occurs when a player creates an intersecting setup that provides two distinct ways to win on the very next turn. Since the opponent can only block one threat per turn, victory is guaranteed.
- **Classic Diagonal Fork**: If Player X controls the top-left and bottom-right corners, and Player O does not control the center, X can claim the bottom-left corner on the third turn. This simultaneously threatens the left vertical line and the bottom horizontal line.
- **Center-Corner Synergy**: If X controls the center and O takes the top-left corner, X should immediately take the bottom-right corner. If O misplays their next move, X can easily create a cross-shaped fork.

## 4. Defensive Playbook
Defense strictly takes priority over offense. If the opponent has two marks in a row, you must immediately block the third square.
- If the opponent takes the Center, you must prioritize taking a Corner.
- **Beware the Diagonal Trap**: If you hold the center and your opponent holds two opposite corners (e.g., top-left and bottom-right), you must NEVER take one of the remaining corners. Doing so allows the opponent to fork you. Instead, you must play on an Edge to force them to block, neutralizing the threat.

## 5. Psychological Warfare & Common Human Errors
The most common mistake human players make is "losing focus" during the second or third move. Specifically, when responding to a Corner opening, humans instinctively tend to claim an adjacent Edge square—a mathematically proven losing move. The AI assistant should remind players to observe board symmetry and calculate defensive lines when they are at a disadvantage.
